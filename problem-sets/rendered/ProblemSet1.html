Do you think mean or median is a better way of representing the average ORF size (and why)? (1 mark)

median is a better way of representing the average ORF size. this is because, as seen from the histogram, the distribution of ORF sizes is right-skewed, thus extremely large but rare ORF sizes will significantly affect the mean, but not the median.

Question 3

Calculate the percentage of Gs and Cs for each ORF (this is called GC content) . How many ORFs have GC content greater than 50%? (2 marks)

yeast_orf_ATGC<- letterFrequency(yeast_orf, c("A", "T", "G", "C"))

yeast_orf_GCcontent<- (yeast_orf_ATGC[,"G"]+ yeast_orf_ATGC[,"C"])/ (yeast_orf_ATGC[,"A"] + yeast_orf_ATGC[,"T"]+ yeast_orf_ATGC[,"G"]+ yeast_orf_ATGC[,"C"])*100

sum(yeast_orf_GCcontent>50)
## [1] 78

Question 5

Plot the distribution of amino acid usage of all yeast ORFs … (i.e how many times is each individual amino acid encoded - you do not need to include STOP codons). (2 marks)

yeast_aa_strings <- translate(yeast_orf) #store amino acid sequences of proteins

aa_list<- c("F","L","S","Y","C","W","P","H","Q","R","I","M","T","N","K","V","A","D","E","G") #store list of amino acids
aa_count_prot <- letterFrequency(yeast_aa_strings, aa_list) #create matrix to store the amino acid count of each protein
aa_count_prot<- as.data.frame(aa_count_prot) #convert matrix into dataframe

aa_count_total= c()
for( i in 1:ncol(aa_count_prot) ){
      aa_count_total= c(aa_count_total,sum(aa_count_prot[,i]))
} #find the total number of each amino acid

barplot(aa_count_total,
        names.arg = aa_list,
        main= "Amino acid usage of all yeast ORFs",
        xlab= "Amino acid",
        ylab= "Frequency",
        ylim= c(0,300000),
        cex.names = 0.6)
